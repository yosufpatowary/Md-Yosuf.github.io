Who I Am

I am an Economics graduate from Government Titumir College, University of Dhaka, with a strong foundation in quantitative research, development economics, and academic writing. My academic journey has been shaped by a deep curiosity about why economies fail the people they are meant to serve — and how rigorous empirical research can guide better policy.

I have worked as a Research Assistant on two government-funded health research projects — one under the Ministry of Planning's Social Science Research Council and another under the Bangladesh Medical Research Council — gaining direct experience in field data collection, research coordination, and ethics-compliant implementation.

As a Research Assistant (Intern) at the Accident Research Institute (ARI), BUET, I drafted technical proposals on road safety and conducted systematic literature reviews. I co-authored a peer-reviewed article published in the Asian Review of Social Sciences (2026), contributing literature synthesis, fieldwork over 400+ respondents, and SPSS/STATA-based statistical analysis.

Beyond research, I have taught undergraduate economics for nearly three years, led the Govt. Titumir College Research Club as Vice President, and founded the 7 College Entrepreneur Forum. I am now actively preparing for graduate study in Development Economics.
